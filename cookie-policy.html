Cookie Policy

Effective: April 20, 2026 · Last updated: April 20, 2026

Cookies are small text files that websites store in your browser. This page explains what cookies pheeew.com uses, why we use them, and how you can control them. For broader information about personal data, see our Privacy Policy.

Categories of cookies we use

Strictly necessary

These cookies are required for the Site to function. They keep items in your cart, remember your session after login, and protect the checkout. They cannot be disabled through our cookie banner, though you can block them through your browser (doing so will likely break checkout).

Functional

These cookies remember your preferences (such as language and previously viewed products) so the Site behaves consistently across visits.

Analytics

These cookies help us understand how visitors use the Site — which pages are popular, where people get stuck, which sources drive subscriptions. We aggregate this data.

Marketing / advertising

If we run paid advertising, these cookies and pixels measure campaign performance and can enable cross-context behavioral advertising. For California residents, this constitutes "sharing" under CPRA — you can opt out on the California Privacy Rights page, or by sending a Global Privacy Control signal from your browser.

Consent and control

On your first visit, we show a cookie banner letting you accept, decline, or customize non-essential cookies. You can revisit these preferences at any time by clicking "Cookie preferences" in the footer. You can also block or delete cookies at the browser level:

  • Safari
  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Edge

Global Privacy Control

PHEEEW honors the Global Privacy Control (GPC) browser signal as a valid opt-out of "sale" and "sharing" of personal information under Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.135.

Legal compliance

This page is published in keeping with the California Online Privacy Protection Act (Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §§ 22575–22579) and the CCPA/CPRA (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100 et seq.). We do not currently target EEA or UK residents; if that changes, we will update this page to reflect GDPR and ePrivacy requirements.
