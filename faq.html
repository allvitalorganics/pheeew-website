FAQ

Before you ask.

Grouped by what it's actually about. Short answers here, longer ones on Science and Our story.

About the brand

What is PHEEEW?

A 100mL puree pouch that starts with the gut. Phase 1 is Gut + Energy — a clean caffeine lift on a real synbiotic base. Gut + Digestion follows, then Gut + Immunity. One architecture, one base, a sequence of functions.

Who built this?

Three friends. Alex Kim (CEO, LA — born in Korea, raised in Seattle), Jason Ko (COO, LA), Dawon Kim (design lead, Seoul). Delaware C-Corp, founded 2026. Read more on Our story.

Where are you based?

Los Angeles for the brand side. Naju, Korea for manufacturing. Seoul for design. Shipped from a 3PL in LA.

Why the name PHEEEW?

It's literally an exhale. The sound you make when something finally works. Also a decent approximation of the noise the pouch makes when you first squeeze one.

Are you a wellness brand?

Not in the way the aisle means it. We don't run BFCM sales, we don't do celebrity endorsements, we don't promise 300% energy boosts. We make a drink that tastes good and happens to have real science in it. If that's wellness, fine — but we'd rather you just try the pouch.

About the product

What's in it?

Real fruit puree. A spore-forming probiotic (Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969). Two prebiotic fibers (XOS and Fibersol-2). For Gut + Energy: green tea extract for natural caffeine, plus B6 and B12. Full breakdown on Science.

How does it taste?

Like a smoothie, not a supplement. Fruit-forward, drinkable, slightly thicker than juice. The Energy SKU lands in the berry family — final flavor profile locks with the pre-launch Nutrition Facts panel.

How much caffeine is in Gut + Energy?

Roughly 50–75mg per pouch, from green tea extract. Less than a cup of coffee, paired with L-theanine that green tea brings naturally. The lift is measured — that's the point.

Does it need refrigeration?

No. The pouch is shelf-stable for twelve months. You can keep it on the counter, in a drawer, in a belt bag. If you want it cold, the fridge works too.

How often should I drink one?

Once a day is the rhythm the Core 30-pack is built for. Some people do the 3pm slot (the afternoon lift), some do the morning (before coffee, with coffee, instead of coffee). There's no wrong answer — it's a food, not a dose.

Is it vegan? Gluten-free? Sugar-free?

Vegan and gluten-free, yes. Sugar-free, no — there's natural fruit sugar from the puree. We don't add sugar. Final grams per pouch on the Nutrition Facts panel.

About ingredients and science

What is a synbiotic?

A product that combines a probiotic (live bacteria) with a prebiotic (fiber that feeds it). Together they do more than either would alone. Full explanation on Science.

Why Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969?

Because it's spore-forming — meaning it survives shelf storage, heat processing, and stomach acid. Most probiotics on shelves are dead by the time you drink them. This one isn't. Deeper read here.

Will it help my bloating/IBS/gut issues?

We can't make that claim about our specific formula — and we wouldn't. The strain has published research on digestive comfort, including an IBS study with meaningful results. Your body is yours; ask a doctor if you have a diagnosed condition.

Is there an age minimum?

We don't recommend Gut + Energy for kids under 12 because of the caffeine. Digestion and Immunity, when they launch, will be caffeine-free and family-friendly.

Can I drink it while pregnant or nursing?

Talk to your doctor. That's not us being cautious — that's genuinely the right call, because caffeine intake during pregnancy is a conversation worth having with someone who knows you.

About the roadmap

When does Gut + Digestion launch?

Phase 2 — after Energy proves out. Roughly six months from Energy launch, earlier if the demand signal is clear. We're not in a hurry.

When does Gut + Immunity launch?

Phase 3 — after Digestion lands. Nine to twelve months from Energy launch. Built for the stretch from late fall through spring.

Why launch Energy first if Digestion is the broader category?

Because Energy is more differentiated. Everyone's doing digestion pouches. Nobody's doing a gut-first caffeine product on a real synbiotic base. Energy gives us a shelf distinction and a reason to be chosen. Digestion will carry the line once we have the trust to support it.
