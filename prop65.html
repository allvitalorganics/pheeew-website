California Proposition 65

Effective: April 20, 2026 · Last updated: April 20, 2026

California Proposition 65 — formally, the Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986 — is a California right-to-know law that requires businesses to provide warnings when a product can expose Californians to chemicals the state has listed as causing cancer, birth defects, or other reproductive harm.

What Proposition 65 is

Prop 65 is codified at California Health & Safety Code § 25249.5 et seq. It is enforced by the California Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment (OEHHA) and by private plaintiffs acting in the public interest. The law requires a "clear and reasonable warning" before knowingly and intentionally exposing a California consumer to a listed chemical above the relevant "safe harbor" exposure level, unless an exemption applies.

A Prop 65 warning does not mean a product is unsafe or illegal, or that it has been recalled. Many common foods contain trace amounts of Prop 65-listed chemicals (for example, acrylamide that forms during heating, or naturally occurring lead in certain plant ingredients) at levels the law requires the producer to disclose.

PHEEEW's current assessment

Assessment status: in progress.

Before we make a first sale to a California consumer, we will complete a Prop 65 exposure assessment for our Gut + Energy pouch covering each ingredient, the contact materials of the pouch itself, and any residuals from manufacturing. This page will be updated with the results, and any required warning will be placed:

  • On the product detail page on pheeew.com (at the point of sale), and
  • On the pouch label and/or outer shipping carton, as required by 27 C.C.R. §§ 25600–25603.

Until that assessment is complete, we are not making any representation that a Prop 65 warning is or is not required for any specific PHEEEW product.

Safe-harbor warning language (for reference)

If the assessment determines a warning is required, we will use the following OEHHA "short-form" safe-harbor warning format (27 C.C.R. § 25603(a)), adapted to the specific chemical at issue:

⚠WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [name of one chemical for which a warning would be required], which is known to the State of California to cause [cancer / birth defects or other reproductive harm]. For more information go to www.P65Warnings.ca.gov.

For food products the warning is governed by the food-specific rule (27 C.C.R. § 25607.2), which permits, among other options, a point-of-sale warning placement.

If no warning is required

If our assessment concludes that no PHEEEW product requires a Prop 65 warning under the current OEHHA Maximum Allowable Dose Levels (MADLs) and No Significant Risk Levels (NSRLs), we will state that here clearly and link to the basis of that conclusion.
