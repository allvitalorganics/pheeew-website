Integrity

What we don't claim — and why that's the point.

By Alex Kim · Apr 2026 · 4 min read

Here's what the wellness aisle has taught the last three generations: promise everything, deliver a feeling, let the label do the math.

We're not doing that.

Gut + Energy is a pouch. It has a synbiotic base — pre and probiotic together. It has clean caffeine from green tea. It has B vitamins stacked on top. It tastes like real fruit because it has real fruit in it. That's the product. That's also the entire claim.

We don't say it cures anything. We don't say it boosts your immune system. We don't say it detoxes, resets, flushes, balances, optimizes, or unlocks. We don't say it will change your skin, your sleep, your mood, or your morning. It might. We hope it does. But we won't say it.

The reason is simple. We want to be around in ten years. You cannot be around in ten years if the thing you sold someone didn't do what the label said.

Promise everything, deliver a feeling, let the label do the math. We're not doing that.

There's a second reason. The people we want to sell to are tired of being lied to. They've bought the shot and the powder and the pill that promised everything and delivered a receipt. They read the ingredient panel. They check the dose. They know the difference between a clinical dose and a pixie-dust dose. They are not who the old category was built for, and they are exactly who this one is.

So here's the line. If a study backs it, and the dose in our pouch matches the dose in the study, we'll say it — carefully, with the citation. If it doesn't, we won't. If someone on staff wants to put a word on the pouch that sounds better than the word the science supports, we won't. If marketing wants a headline that's fifteen percent more aggressive than what we can defend, we won't.

The short version: we claim less than we could, so the things we do claim land harder.

A smoothie for your gut. Clean caffeine. Real synbiotic base. Lift, not jitters. Taste, not chalk.

That's it. That's the whole pitch. If it turns out to do more than that for you, come tell us. We'll write you back.

— Alex
