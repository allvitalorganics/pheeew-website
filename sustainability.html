Sustainability

How we think about sustainability.

We're not going to greenwash. Here's what we actually do, what we don't do yet, and what we're working on.

What we do

  • Lighter transport. A 100 mL pouch weighs less per serving than a bottled equivalent, which means less fuel moving it from factory to shelf to your door.
  • Recyclable pouch material. Our current pouch uses a recyclable laminate spec'd with our Korean manufacturer. Check your local stream — not all curbside programs accept flexible packaging yet.
  • Carbon-aware shipping. Our 3PL routes orders via the lowest-footprint carrier available for each lane. Regional-first where possible.
  • Minimal secondary packaging. No oversized boxes. No plastic fillers. Recycled and recyclable paper on everything outside the pouch.
  • No sample-pack bloat. We don't ship promo throwaways. You get the pouch you paid for and a short thank-you note.

What we don't do yet

  • We don't have a compostable pouch option at scale. The current compostable laminates on the market can't hold up to shelf life and shipping temps yet. When they can, we'll move.
  • We don't run a carbon offset program at launch. We'd rather reduce before we offset, and we haven't earned the right to make an offset claim without the reduction data to back it.
  • We are not B Corp certified. It's on the roadmap, but we're a three-person company that hasn't shipped a unit yet. We'll pursue it when we can do it honestly.
  • We don't publish a full lifecycle footprint. We will once we have real sales data to measure against.

What we're working on

  • Pouch v2. A fully recyclable or compostable spec, targeted for the next manufacturing run after launch validates.
  • Shipping carbon labels. Rough per-order footprint estimates shown at checkout, once we have enough lane data to not make something up.
  • B Corp pursuit once we clear the revenue threshold and the operational maturity gates.
  • Take-back pilot. Small local pilot for LA customers to return pouches at markets, if the numbers make sense.

Ingredient sourcing. The pouch is manufactured in Korea. Ingredient origins — fruit, probiotic strain, caffeine source — will be disclosed in full post-launch on about · science. We'll name origins we can verify, and we'll say "don't know yet" for the ones we can't.

If you find a claim on this page we can't back up, email hello@pheeew.com and we'll fix it. That rule applies to the whole site.
