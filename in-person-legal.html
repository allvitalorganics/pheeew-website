In-Person / Farmers Market Legal Pack

Effective: April 20, 2026 · Last updated: April 20, 2026 · Intended audience: PHEEEW booth team + market regulators

This is the operational legal reference for every PHEEEW farmers-market, pop-up, or in-person event. Print it, laminate it, post it at the booth, and carry a copy in the crate. If a market manager, health inspector, or customer asks for paperwork, this is the top layer — the underlying permits and certificates live in the ops binder.

2. Insurance

  • Product Liability Insurance — minimum $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate recommended. Many CA farmers markets require evidence of coverage before allowing a food booth.
  • General Liability Insurance — standard for in-person events.
  • Workers' Compensation — required in California for any employee (Cal. Labor Code § 3700), including part-time booth staff.
  • Carry a printed Certificate of Insurance with the specific market operator named as additional insured.

3. Food handler certifications

Every person who handles open food at a PHEEEW booth must hold, and carry proof of, at least one of the following:

  • California Food Handler Card — required of most food-handling employees under Cal. Health & Safety Code § 113947.1 (introduced by SB 602), issued by an ANSI-accredited trainer. Valid for three years.
  • ServSafe Food Handler or equivalent — accepted in most California jurisdictions where the Food Handler Card is required.
  • At least one person on site who has a current Food Protection Manager Certification is recommended under § 113947, particularly when sampling is conducted.

4. Labeling requirements (every pouch, every sales carton)

PHEEEW pouches are sold as conventional food (beverage), so federal labeling follows 21 C.F.R. Part 101. Every pouch must show:

  1. Statement of identity — the common name of the product ("Gut + Energy synbiotic beverage" or similar) on the principal display panel (21 C.F.R. § 101.3).
  2. Net quantity of contents — "100 mL (3.4 fl oz)" in both metric and US customary units (21 C.F.R. § 101.105; FPLA at 15 U.S.C. § 1453).
  3. Ingredient list in descending order of predominance by weight, using common or usual names (21 C.F.R. § 101.4).
  4. Nutrition Facts panel following the 2016-current format (21 C.F.R. § 101.9), with serving size expressed per RACC.
  5. Allergen statement. The nine major food allergens must be declared where present (Food Allergen Labeling and Consumer Protection Act, 21 U.S.C. § 343 / FALCPA, as amended by the FASTER Act of 2021 adding sesame): milk, eggs, fish, Crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, and sesame. Declare in a "Contains" statement or parentheticals.
  6. Name and address of the responsible party — "Manufactured for PHEEEW, Inc., Los Angeles, CA" (21 C.F.R. § 101.5).
  7. Country of origin — "Product of Korea" (19 U.S.C. § 1304).
  8. Storage instruction — "Shelf-stable. Refrigerate after opening and consume promptly."
  9. Best-by date — printed lot + expiry on pouch.
  10. Bioengineered food disclosure if any regulated ingredient applies (7 C.F.R. Part 66, USDA AMS). For our current formula, confirm with the contract manufacturer before first production; if no disclosure is required, document the basis.

Keep a current approved label file with the contract manufacturer on file in the ops binder. Do not sell any pouch with a label that deviates from the approved version.

5. Claims rules — what we can and can't say at the booth

PHEEEW is classified as a conventional food, not a dietary supplement. We do not make DSHEA-style structure/function claims, and we do not make FDA-authorized health claims without filing. Applicable authorities: FDA nutrient-content claim rule (21 C.F.R. § 101.13), health-claim rule (21 C.F.R. § 101.14), and the FTC Truth-in-Advertising framework (FTC Act § 12, 15 U.S.C. § 52).

OK to say

  • "A 100mL smoothie pouch."
  • "Contains Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 [X] billion CFU per pouch" (if the spec on the pouch matches).
  • "Green tea caffeine — [X] mg per pouch."
  • "Shelf-stable. No refrigeration needed before opening."
  • "Made in Naju, South Korea for PHEEEW in Los Angeles."
  • "A clean format for a real food with a synbiotic base."

Do not say

  • Any claim that PHEEEW treats, cures, prevents, or diagnoses any disease (that would make it an unapproved drug under 21 U.S.C. § 321(g)).
  • "FDA approved" — FDA does not approve conventional foods.
  • Any specific health-outcome claim ("lowers cholesterol," "fixes IBS," "boosts immunity") without the underlying FDA-authorized claim + qualifying criteria.
  • Weight-loss or cognitive-performance claims.
  • Any comparative superiority claim we cannot substantiate (FTC § 5).
  • Anything the science page and approved copy deck don't already say.

When in doubt, say: "We're a food. If you want the detail, here's our science page." Then point to the card.

6. Sample & sale procedure

  1. Check in with the market manager on arrival. Present TFF permit and COI.
  2. Set up within the assigned footprint. Tent, 10'×10' max unless the market allows larger.
  3. Sample service follows California Retail Food Code (Cal. Health & Safety Code § 113700 et seq.):
    • Use single-use sample cups (≤2 fl oz) — no shared cups.
    • Sample server's hands: handwashing before service, gloves changed at least hourly, never touch the lip of the cup.
    • Sample product must come from an unopened pouch opened at the booth.
    • Once a pouch is opened it must be used within the same market day and discarded; unused samples go into a covered trash.
    • Keep opened pouches in a shaded, clean prep area; product is shelf-stable, but never re-cap and re-use across days.
  4. Sale transactions run through Square on the booth tablet; receipts are offered to every customer (email or printed).
  5. Cash: accepted, counted twice, drop bag secured between transactions. End-of-day reconciliation logged in booth spreadsheet.

7. Sanitation & set-up (CA Retail Food Code highlights)

  • Handwashing. A handwashing setup with warm running water, soap, and single-use towels is required for any booth that opens product (Cal. Health & Safety Code §§ 114332.3, 113953). A 5-gallon camp-style hand-wash with catch basin meets the standard used by most LA-County farmers markets; confirm with the market's own rule.
  • Overhead protection. A canopy or tent is required whenever food is opened outdoors (§ 114335).
  • Surfaces. Smooth, cleanable tables — no bare wood. Covered waste container.
  • Temperature. PHEEEW pouches are shelf-stable; if we ever sell chilled, time-and-temperature control rules apply (§ 113996).
  • Tobacco, alcohol, animals. None at the booth during service.
  • Illness rule. Any team member experiencing vomiting, diarrhea, jaundice, sore throat with fever, or an open infected wound within 24 hours before a shift does not work the booth (§ 113949).

8. Sales tax & receipts

California Sales and Use Tax applies to the sale of most beverages. Under Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 6359, certain food products for human consumption are exempt; however, non-carbonated or carbonated beverages with added sweeteners, or certain functional-beverage categories, may be taxable. Our default operating assumption: PHEEEW is taxable in California. Collect sales tax at the destination-district rate (for LA booth operations, the LA County combined rate) configured in Square. Confirm tax status with our accountant before launch.

Receipts: offer a receipt on every sale. Save daily Square summaries; cash sales logged manually. CDTFA requires retention of sales records for four years (Cal. Rev. & Tax. Code § 7053).

11. Photo & video consent

  • Booth capture of the booth itself, the team, the pouches, and the crowd in aggregate is permitted in most public-market settings under normal public-space expectations.
  • Do not capture identifiable minors (roughly, anyone reading as under 18) without verbal consent from a parent or guardian on camera.
  • If a customer asks not to be filmed, stop filming in their direction and delete any direct footage.
  • For customer testimonials or on-camera interviews: before pressing record, read the release: "Hey — can I grab 30 seconds of you saying what you think? If yes, I'll keep this clip and maybe use it on our socials." Their "yes" on camera is your consent record. For anything longer or more produced, use the signed release form in the ops binder.
  • California is a two-party consent state for audio recording in private settings (Cal. Penal Code § 632); public spaces are lower-risk but still require a reasonable expectation check.

12. Customer incident protocol

If a customer reports an allergic reaction, slip, burn, contamination, or other incident:

  1. Safety first. If the customer is in distress, call 911. Do not diagnose. If the booth has a basic first-aid kit, offer it.
  2. Notify the market manager immediately. Most markets require the manager to be looped in.
  3. Document. Fill out the incident form (in the ops binder):
    • Date, time, booth location.
    • Customer name and contact (if they consent).
    • What happened, in their words and in staff's words.
    • Photos of the product / sample cup / area.
    • Lot number of the pouch sampled or sold.
    • Staff on duty, signed.
  4. Escalate to Alex Kim (CEO) and Jason Ko (COO) within the hour. Phone, not just text.
  5. Preserve evidence. Do not dispose of the product, sample cup, or related materials. Bag and label with the incident form.
  6. Do not admit liability. Express concern, offer to help, but do not say "we caused this" or "we'll pay your bills" on the spot. Insurance and counsel handle those decisions.
  7. Follow up with the customer the next business day.
